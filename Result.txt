*********Moderate
Psychotherapy 

Interpersonal Therapy (IPT): Focuses on improving relationships and addressing conflicts or challenges in social connections.
Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT): Helps identify negative thought patterns and replace them with healthier, constructive thinking.

Antidepressant Medications

SSRIs (Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors): Medications like sertraline and escitalopram can help alleviate depressive symptoms by increasing serotonin levels in the brain.
MAOIs (Monoamine Oxidase Inhibitors): Medications such as tranylcypromine, phenelzine, and isocarboxazid, though effective, require careful diet management due to potential side effects.

Exercise Habit

Recommendation: Regular physical activity can prevent mild depression from worsening and help manage moderate depression symptoms.

Dietary Habit

Recommendation: Maintain a balanced, healthy diet rich in nutrients to support overall mental well-being.

Relaxation Techniques

Recommendation: Techniques such as progressive muscle relaxation, music therapy, or yoga can reduce stress and help manage moderate depression.

*********Severe

Set Realistic Goals

Recommendation: Break larger tasks into smaller, manageable steps and set achievable goals to avoid feeling overwhelmed and increase productivity.

Recommendation: Engage in physical activities to boost mood, improve sleep, and increase energy levels, which can help alleviate severe depressive symptoms.

Manage Stress

Recommendation: Practice stress-reduction techniques such as yoga, meditation, or journaling to manage daily stress and improve mental well-being.

Consider Therapy

Recommendation: Therapy, whether in-person, online, or via a digital program, can help regain control and satisfaction in life by addressing the root causes of depression.

Medication and Brain Stimulation

Recommendation: Antidepressants can be prescribed for treatment, and in cases where medications are ineffective, brain stimulation therapies may be considered.
